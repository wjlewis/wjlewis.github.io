Dear folks at Codecademy,

I experienced several false starts in my initial attempts to learn how to program. Everything I found online was pitched at too high a level for me, and I didn't know anyone in the field to ask for help. So it's without exaggeration that I say that discovering Codecademy one summer marked a turning point in my intellectual and professional life. I worked through the Python lessons over the course of the following month, and shortly thereafter I was hooked. I'd always enjoyed learning new skills, taking objects apart to see how they're constructed, and building things. I soon realized that programming offered all of these possibilities and more. Here was a medium that was completely free, practically limitless, and creatively unconstrained. It was nothing short of an epiphany.

Codecademy introduced me to what has become a career and a favorite pastime. I would be honored to work with a team whose aim is to kindle that same interest in others.

Best,
